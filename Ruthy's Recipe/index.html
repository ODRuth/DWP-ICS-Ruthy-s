Recipes Special Ghanaian Dishes

Banku And Tilapia

Banku and Tilapia is a traditional Ghanaian dish that is very common amongst all regions in Ghana. Banku is basically a mixture of corn dough mixed with cassava dough. It can be eaten with various sauces such as stew, hot pepper sauce, soup etc. Tilapia is a river fish that is a delicacy in Ghana. It can be used for stew, soups and also eaten by grilling, baking and frying. Enjoy!!

Ingredients

  • ½ pack of Corn Mix /dough
  • ½ pack of Cassava flour/dough
  • 2 cups of Water
  • 1 tbsp. of Salt
  • 1 tsp of Salt
  • 2 big sized Maggie cube
  • Seasoning salt of choice

Waakye

Rice and beans is a popular and cheap combination for a meal in different cultures. Ghanaian waakye is the country’s version of this combination. This humble dish, which is eaten any time of the day is cooked with red sorghum leaf sheath, known as “waakye leaves”, which gives it a burgundy (reddish-brown) colour

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of rice
  • 1 ½ cup beans or any kind of beans peas
  • salt
  • 4 dry sorghum leaves
  • 10 cups of water

Rice balls and Peanut butter soup

Nkate nkwan(Peanut butter soup)is one of Ghana’s most famous national dishes. It is a soup made from peanuts. Many people refer to it as peanut butter soup, peanut or groundnut soup. There is a wide variety of peanut soups and stews across West Africa. It is almost always served with omo tuo(Rice balls), but also goes very well with any kind of fufu.

Ingredients

  • medium onions or 1 large one
  • ripe large tomatoes
  • A whole chicken, chopped to pieces (we like "boiler" chicken, free range, "koko ya Setswana")
  • Garden eggs (optional)
  • okra (with ends trimmed)
  • scotch bonnet pepper or kpakpo shito
  • Ginger
  • bay leaves
  • rosemary sprig
  • 1.75l chicken stock
  • tbsp peanut butter
  • Water to mix with the peanut butter

Black eyed beans stew and friend plantain

Ghanaian black eyed beans stew popularly known as red red is made by mixing cooked beans with tomato sauce and served with fried sweet plantains

Ingredients

  • medium tomatoes
  • tablespoons of tomato purée
  • large onions (chopped)
  • cloves of pressed garlic
  • red palm oil or vegetable oil
  • seasoning cube
  • scotch bonnet chilli (optional)
  • teaspoon of ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 300g black-eyed peas or red beans

Ghana Jollof rice and spicy chicken

Ghanaian Jollof Rice is a dish that’s cooked to perfection, and it is curated to bring you the brightly colored orange/red dish with the most tantalizing spices and flavor. It definitely was a staple in my house growing up, and at parties, where you can rest assure that there will be pans and pans of it. This is the first reason I love attending Ghanaian parties.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 large red onion (6 to 7 ounces)
  • 4 cups uncooked jasmine or basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt, plus more as needed
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme, or 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 cups beef, chicken, or vegetable broth

Apapransa and Crabs

Apapransa is a local meal with its origin from the Ga Adangbe. Aprapransa, originally called ‘Akplijii’ is a Ghanaian GaDangme dish prepared by heat mixing roasted corn flour with a sacred GaDangme soup called ‘Palm Nut Soup. It is a meal that is not commonly found outside. It is a rather interesting dish which is delicious too. It can be eaten in the morning or afternoon.

Ingredients

  • 1 bag of palmnuts
  • 1 pound of meat (steamed)
  • 3 round large crabs
  • Herrings
  • 1 onion
  • 4 tomatoes
  • 3 garden eggs
  • 4 scotch bonnet pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of ground ginger
  • Roasted corn flour
  • Red beans (boiled)
